let's learn about WEIGHT

what is Weight?

Weights are a measure of the strength between two neurons. They scale from 0 to 1. The higher the weight, the more influence the input neuron will have on its associated output neuron. For example, a neuron with a weight of 0.9 will be incredibly influential on the output, while a neuron with a weight of 0.02 will not have a substantial imapact.

Weights are separate from biases, which are not impacted by the values of the past layer of neurons. Unlike weights, biases are always set to 1, to allow the activation of further layers even if the previous neurons and weights all equate to 0. While a bias cannot be impacted by incoming weights, later layers can shape it.
